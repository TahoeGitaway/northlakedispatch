# Productivity — Season-over-Season (Year-over-Year)

_Comparing the two 365-day scans, focused only on the months that exist in **both** years._

## Why January–May is the only "golden" window

- **Current scan:** 2025-06-26 → 2026-06-26 → gives full months Jul 2025 … May 2026.
- **Prior scan:** 2024-06-25 → 2025-06-25, but counted data only really begins **Jan 2025** (these people weren't logging `finished_by` completions before then).
- **Overlap of calendar months present in both years = January, February, March, April, May.**
  - Jan–May 2025 (prior) vs Jan–May 2026 (current): full months on both sides → apples to apples.
  - **June is excluded from the core read** — 2025 June is split across the two files (1–25 in prior, 26–30 in current) and 2026 June is only 26 days. It's shown at the bottom as a directional footnote, not as golden data.
  - Jul–Dec only exist in one year → no fair comparison.

**Why same-month matters:** task counts reflect how much work *existed* that month, not just effort. Winter (ski/holiday) is the busy peak; April–May is the slow shoulder. Comparing Jan vs Jan, May vs May controls for that — a May number is only meaningful against another May.

## The seasonal shape (both years agree)

- **Peak: Jan–Mar** (winter — holidays + ski season). Everyone's biggest months.
- **Trough: Apr–May** (mud/shoulder season). Counts fall because there's simply less turnover work.

So Jan–May captures one full peak *and* one full trough in each year — a fair span.

## What it says about each person

- **Andy — up sharply (+26%).** The standout. His weak spots last year filled in: January jumped +78 and his dismal April 2025 (58, the lowest single month of anyone) nearly doubled to 130. Gains are broad, not one fluke month. Trending the right way across the whole span.
- **Irving — steady climber (+10%).** Already a high performer and still added 82. His growth came in the *shoulder* months (May +50, Apr +12) — he's getting more done in the slow season, which is the harder place to grow. Only soft spot was March.
- **Chris — top output, modestly up (+7%).** Highest Jan–May total both years (911). His shape shifted: a huge January (+117) offset softer Feb/Mar. Net positive, but more front-loaded into peak season than Irving's.
- **Trevor — flat (+0.8%).** The one to watch. Total barely moved. Strong April (+52) was cancelled by drops in March (−39) and May (−31). Not declining, but not growing while the team rose ~10% around him — so relatively he slipped.
